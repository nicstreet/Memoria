# AI & Face Detection — Settings Guide

---

## Face Recognition Model

The neural network used to convert a detected face into a list of numbers (an "embedding") that mathematically represents what that face looks like. The app then compares embeddings to decide if two faces are the same person.

| Model | Accuracy | Speed | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ArcFace** *(default)* | ★★★★★ | Medium | Best overall accuracy. Recommended for most libraries. |
| **Facenet512** | ★★★★☆ | Medium | Strong alternative to ArcFace; 512-dimension embeddings. |
| **VGG-Face** | ★★★☆☆ | Slow | Older model; generally outperformed by ArcFace. |
| **DeepFace** | ★★★☆☆ | Fast | Lightweight; less accurate on varied lighting/angles. |
| **OpenFace** | ★★☆☆☆ | Fast | Oldest model; lowest accuracy. |

> **Important:** Changing the face recognition model requires a full re-scan of all photos. Embeddings generated by different models are not compatible with each other.

## Face Detector

The algorithm used to *locate* faces within a photo before the recognition model runs. A missed detection means a face is never processed at all.

| Detector | Accuracy | Speed | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **RetinaFace** *(default)* | ★★★★★ | Slow | Best overall. Handles small, angled, and partially obscured faces well. |
| **MTCNN** | ★★★★☆ | Medium | Good alternative; often better on group shots and varied angles. |
| **OpenCV** | ★★★☆☆ | Fast | Basic Haar cascade. Works well on clear, front-facing photos. |
| **SSD** | ★★★☆☆ | Fast | Single-shot detector; reasonable accuracy at speed. |
| **dlib** | ★★★☆☆ | Medium | Reliable on clean portrait-style photos; struggles with small faces. |

> **Tip:** If faces in your library are frequently missed (especially in group shots or candid photos), switching from RetinaFace to **MTCNN** often helps.

> **Important:** Changing the detector requires a full re-scan of all photos.

## Face Match Sensitivity

Controls how closely a face must resemble a known, *named* person to be automatically assigned to them. This runs *after* clustering, during the matching pass.

The value is a **maximum distance** — lower means stricter matching.

| Setting | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Lower (e.g. 0.45)** | Stricter — only very confident matches are accepted. Fewer false assignments but may leave more faces unassigned. |
| **Default (0.60)** | Balanced — works well for clear photos with reasonable variation in lighting and expression. |
| **Higher (e.g. 0.75)** | More lenient — catches more matches but increases the risk of assigning the wrong person to a face. |

> **Tip:** If the wrong person keeps being assigned to faces, lower this value. If too many faces are being left unassigned when you'd expect a match, raise it slightly.

---

## Cluster Grouping Sensitivity

Controls how the app groups *unknown* faces together into clusters before any names are assigned. Two faces are placed in the same cluster when their distance is within this threshold.

The value is a **maximum distance** — lower means tighter groups.

| Setting | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Lower (e.g. 0.25–0.30)** | Tighter clusters — faces must be very similar to be grouped together. Produces more clusters, each with higher confidence. Risk: the same person may be split across multiple clusters (e.g. different lighting or angles). |
| **Default (0.40)** | Balanced — works well for clear, front-facing photos with moderate variation. |
| **Higher (e.g. 0.55–0.60)** | Looser clusters — faces can differ more and still be grouped together. Produces fewer, larger clusters. Risk: different people may be merged into the same cluster. |

> **Tip:** Start by lowering this value if clusters seem to contain mixed people. Raise it if the same person keeps appearing as many separate clusters.

---

## Minimum Cluster Size

The minimum number of faces required for a cluster to be kept. Clusters smaller than this are treated as **noise** and discarded — they won't appear for naming.

| Setting | Effect |
|---|---|
| **1** | Every detected face is kept, including one-off detections that may be false positives. |
| **2** *(default)* | A face must appear at least twice to form a cluster. Filters out most accidental detections. |
| **3–5** | Stricter filtering — only people who appear frequently in the library will form clusters. Useful for large libraries with many strangers in the background. |

> **Tip:** For a personal family library, keep this at 2. For event photography with many unknown faces, raising to 3 or 4 reduces noise significantly.

---

## Recommended Starting Point if Detection is Poor

1. **Switch detector to `mtcnn`** if faces are frequently missed.
2. **Lower cluster threshold to 0.30** for tighter, more trustworthy clusters.
3. **Lower match threshold to 0.50** if wrong people are being assigned.
4. After changing settings, run **Edit → Re-assess** to reprocess with the new values.
5. For a completely clean slate (e.g. after changing the model or detector), faces need to be fully re-scanned — this can be done via the CLI: `python main.py scan-faces`.
